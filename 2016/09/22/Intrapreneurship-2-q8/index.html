Adding a New Product to Your Business (a.k.a Intrapreneurship) - Top 10 Questions - Part 2 Question 8

2016-09-22

Thoughts on the question “What will be the impact on existing customers, products, operations, etc.?” introduced in Part 1:

How will existing operations be impacted? Does your organization have enough operational redundancy to absorb any shocks caused by the new product? Or, will your organization require significantly different capabilities potentially causing misalignment, increased variability and change in political landscape?

How about the potential effects of your new product on your existing products and customers? While it may be okay to cannibalize a certain percentage of your existing business, be careful not to take away too much from a business line (whether it is in revenues or resources) that has been working well for your company. The new product should strengthen your portfolio and customer base, not weaken it.

If the new product is not complementary to the existing product, but is aligned well with the (new) goals of the company, you will need to move existing customers to the new product when it is completed. If your pro forma depends heavily on this move, you need to identify an upgrade path that makes economic sense for both the buyer and the seller. If you cannot figure this out, your proposal may not be feasible.
