Adding a New Product to Your Business (a.k.a Intrapreneurship) - Top 10 Questions - Part 2 Question 2

2016-09-22

Thoughts on the question “What are the required core competencies?” introduced in Part 1:

Your proposal may address customer pain, but your company may not be best positioned to build the product to address that pain. Do you have a team that has a visceral understanding of the domain and the necessary setup (whether it is your value chain, systems, partners, etc) or will you need to build the relevant core competencies on-the-fly, while trying to execute faster than the incumbents and the new entrants of your new product’s industry?

Involving the right people and building the best team is often an afterthought in new business proposals. But this part of your plan could make or brake your project. After all, a great team can take a mediocre idea and make it successful, while a mediocre team can take a great idea and run it to the ground. Think through the details of who you need on the team. A very common tendency is to overload existing team members with additional work and responsibilities, which risks not only the success of your new product but has a detrimental effect on existing products/operations as well. Also, if at all possible, do not sacrifice proper UX research. If you are concerned you will break the budget, consider this; the right investment now, will reduce your risk and increase your chances of expected return. Do not short-change your product’s potential; build the best team you can.
